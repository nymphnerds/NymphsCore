Guide 03

Refine with guide images.Push the design closer to intention.

Use the editing stage when the first image is close but not right yet. This is where you tighten the design, steer details, and reduce the distance between the rough reference and the actual character or asset you want.

// Walkthrough

Use editing to correct, not restart.

This stage works best when you already have a strong base image. Use it to move the image toward the design you meant, not to rescue something with no clear direction at all.

Step 01

Start from your strongest base image

Bring in the version that already has the best silhouette, attitude, or structure. Editing goes further when the base already has something worth preserving.

Step 02

Guide one change at a time

When possible, push the design in deliberate passes instead of asking one edit to solve everything at once.

  • Adjust costume direction.
  • Correct anatomy or proportion drift.
  • Tighten face, material, or silhouette language.
Step 03

Look for design clarity

The best edited result is not just prettier. It is easier to understand and easier to use as the basis for later extraction or 3D work.

Step 04

Save the version that resolves the confusion

When one pass finally makes the design make sense, stop there and carry that version forward.

// Best Use

Editing is for direction control.

Think of this stage as bringing the image under control. It is where a nearly-good image becomes a usable one.

// Avoid

Do not pile on random fixes.

If the base image has no stable direction, restart earlier instead of layering vague corrections onto a weak foundation.
