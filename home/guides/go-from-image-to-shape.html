Guide 04

Go from image to shape.Carry the strongest input forward.

This is where the image stops being just a concept reference and starts becoming a 3D draft. The goal is not a final production asset. The goal is a believable first 3D pass you can evaluate and keep building from.

Read the stepsWatch the demo slotBack to guide hub
// Walkthrough

Feed the cleanest image into the 3D stage.

Image-to-shape works best when your source image is already doing a clear job. If the image is muddy, the 3D result often inherits that uncertainty.

Step 01

Choose the strongest input image

Use the image with the clearest silhouette, readable pose, and most useful surface clues. This is not the time to pick the weirdest or most experimental image.

Step 02

Set expectations correctly

The first 3D pass is a draft. Judge it by whether it preserves the major form and gives you something workable, not by whether it looks like a final game asset.

Step 03

Watch the handoff, not just the output

Pay attention to whether the app makes the transition into shape generation feel understandable. The process should feel deliberate, not like a black box jump.

  • Was it clear which image was being used?
  • Was it obvious that the process had started?
  • Did the resulting draft match the intent of the source image closely enough?
Step 04

Decide whether to continue or step back

If the draft is close, continue into texture or cleanup. If the draft is confused, step back and strengthen the image stage rather than forcing a weak base further forward.

// Good Results

Look for structural trust.

A good first shape preserves the core read of the source image. It does not need to be perfect to be useful.

// Warning Sign

If the image is unclear, the shape often will be too.

When the 3D result feels random, the most valuable fix is often earlier in the pipeline, not later.
