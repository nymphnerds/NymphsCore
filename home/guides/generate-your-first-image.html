Guide 02

Generate your first image.Start with a useful reference.

This guide is about getting a result that is actually helpful for the rest of the pipeline. Treat the first image as the foundation for later editing, extraction, or shape work.

Read the stepsWatch the demo slotBack to guide hub
// Walkthrough

Aim for a clean first result.

The point of the first image is not to prove that the button works. It is to create a reference you would actually want to continue with.

Step 01

Pick one clear subject

Keep the first run focused. One readable subject with a strong silhouette is better than a crowded prompt with too many goals.

Step 02

Use image generation to establish direction

Look for overall shape, attitude, costume direction, and clarity. This is where you decide whether the design is worth refining further.

Step 03

Keep the best candidate, not all of them

Once you have one image that feels close, treat it as the branch you continue from. The rest of the workflow gets easier when you commit to a direction.

  • Pick the clearest silhouette.
  • Prefer readable anatomy and costume separation.
  • Save the one that makes later editing easiest.
Step 04

Decide the next move

If the image is close but not there yet, move into editing. If the design is already strong and modular enough, it may be ready for extraction or shape work.

// Good First Results

Readable beats flashy.

A strong first image is one you can keep working from. Clean forms and clear separation matter more than trying to make the first pass final.

// If It Misses

Do not force a bad base image.

If the subject or proportions are fighting you already, reroll or re-prompt before you sink time into later stages.
